Programming Language

If you want your game to actually work, you’ll need to put some time into programming. This is often one of the most technical aspects of game development, so it’s important to have at least a basic understanding of common programming languages like C++ , Unreal Blueprints ,C# or UnityScript.

Art and Asset Creation

In order for your game to look and feel its best, you’ll need to create high-quality art assets. This includes everything from 2D sprites and 3D models to user interface elements and background scenery.

Testing

Once you have a playable build of your game, it’s time to start testing. This includes playtesting your game internally to iron out any bugs or glitches, as well as conducting external beta tests with larger groups of players.

Ideas and Creativity

This is where it all begins – coming up with the initial idea for your game. Once you have an idea, it’s important to start fleshing it out by asking yourself key questions such as “What kind of gameplay experience do I want to create?”, “What kind of story do I want to tell?”, “Who is my target audience?” etc.
